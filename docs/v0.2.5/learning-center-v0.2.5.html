Find Your Grade Path

Each grade band offers a structured scope and sequence built around core academic skills and STEM integration. Select the band that fits your student to learn what to expect.

Elementary

Grades 3–5

Students in this band strengthen core literacy and numeracy while developing their first systematic encounters with scientific thinking and mathematical reasoning. The focus is on building accuracy, persistence, and curiosity as foundational habits.

  • Reading comprehension and informational text analysis
  • Multi-digit arithmetic, fractions, and early geometry
  • Introduction to the scientific method and data observation
  • Writing with structure: sentences, paragraphs, and evidence
Explore Grades 3–5
Middle School

Grades 6–8

Middle school learners encounter increasingly abstract concepts across disciplines. This band supports the transition to pre-algebraic and algebraic thinking, structured writing, and disciplinary science, while helping students develop independent study strategies.

  • Pre-algebra, algebra fundamentals, and proportional reasoning
  • Life, earth, and physical science with lab-based inquiry
  • Expository and argumentative writing across content areas
  • Study skills: note-taking, test preparation, and organization
Explore Grades 6–8
High School

Grades 9–12

High school students work toward subject-area mastery, standardized assessment readiness, and the academic skills that support post-secondary preparation. STEM coursework is supported at every level from Algebra II through AP sciences and beyond.

  • Geometry, Algebra II, Pre-calculus, and Calculus support
  • Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and AP STEM course alignment
  • SAT/ACT preparation: quantitative and verbal reasoning
  • Research writing, source evaluation, and academic argumentation

Getting Started

How the Assessment Works

Before recommending a program, we take time to understand each student through a structured skills assessment. The process is straightforward, low-pressure, and designed to give families clear, actionable information.

45–60 minute session In-center or virtual No cost at intake Grades 3–12
1

Initial Consultation

A brief conversation with a Learning Center advisor to understand your student’s current course load, challenges, and goals. Takes approximately 15–20 minutes by phone or in person.

2

Skills Assessment

A structured, written assessment covering the relevant subject areas for your student’s grade band. Administered at the center or via a supervised virtual session. No preparation is needed.

3

Learning Plan Review

Families receive a written summary of results, identified skill gaps, and a recommended program and session schedule. Reviewed together with an advisor before enrollment is discussed.

Elementary · Grades 3–5

Programs & Services for Grades 3–5

At the elementary level, the Learning Center focuses on building the academic foundation that all future learning depends upon. Sessions are structured, encouraging, and paced to meet each student where they are—while steadily expanding what they can do independently.

Core Subject Tutoring

One-on-one or small-group sessions targeting reading comprehension, writing mechanics, and foundational mathematics. Instructors align work to grade-level standards and classroom curriculum.

1-on-1 or Small Group 50-min sessions Math · ELA
STEM Exploration Workshop

Structured hands-on activities introduce students to observation, measurement, and basic data recording through guided science and math challenges. Designed to develop curiosity and careful thinking.

Small Group 60-min sessions Science · Math
Reading & Literacy Support

Targeted intervention for students who need additional support with decoding, fluency, or reading comprehension. Sessions use structured literacy approaches with progress tracking shared with families.

1-on-1 50-min sessions ELA · Literacy
Individualized Learning Plan Review

Each enrolled student receives an initial skills assessment and a written learning plan. Families receive a summary of current performance, target areas, and a recommended session schedule.

Family Consultation One-time / Quarterly

What Families Can Expect

  • Written skills assessment at enrollment
  • Monthly progress summaries shared with parents
  • Homework support integrated into sessions
  • Consistent instructor assignment when possible
  • Flexible scheduling — afternoons and weekends
  • Clear, jargon-free communication at every step

Not sure where to start? An assessment helps us recommend the right program.

Middle School · Grades 6–8

Programs & Services for Grades 6–8

Middle school introduces students to more abstract reasoning, disciplinary science, and the organizational demands of multiple teachers and subjects. Our programs help students build the skills and self-awareness needed to manage that transition effectively.

Subject-Area Tutoring

Focused support in mathematics (pre-algebra through algebra), life and physical sciences, and English language arts. Sessions can be aligned to specific assignments, units, or assessments.

1-on-1 or Small Group 50-min sessions Math · Science · ELA
Algebra Readiness Program

A structured progression designed to close gaps in pre-algebraic reasoning and build confidence before or during Algebra I. Covers ratios, proportional relationships, equations, and functions.

Small Group Multi-week program Mathematics
STEM Lab Sessions

Inquiry-based sessions that connect classroom science content to hands-on data collection and analysis. Students practice forming hypotheses, recording results, and drawing evidence-based conclusions.

Small Group 75-min sessions Science · Math
Study Skills & Executive Function Coaching

Students develop practical strategies for note-taking, assignment management, test preparation, and time planning. Sessions are especially useful during transitions between grade levels.

1-on-1 50-min sessions Organization · Planning

What Families Can Expect

  • Diagnostic assessment aligned to current grade standards
  • Bi-weekly progress notes for parents and guardians
  • Coordination with school curriculum where possible
  • Standardized test readiness introduced in Grade 8
  • Evening and weekend availability
  • End-of-term written progress report

An initial assessment helps us place your student in the right program and level.

High School · Grades 9–12

Programs & Services for Grades 9–12

High school students face rigorous coursework, high-stakes assessments, and increasing pressure to demonstrate academic readiness. Our programs provide structured, content-specific support designed to meet those demands without shortcuts.

Advanced Subject Tutoring

Expert support in high school mathematics (Geometry through Calculus), biology, chemistry, and physics—including AP-level courses. Sessions are paced to the student’s syllabus and upcoming assessments.

1-on-1 60-min sessions Math · Science · AP
SAT / ACT Preparation

A structured preparation program covering both quantitative and verbal reasoning sections. Includes timed practice, error analysis, and strategy review. Available as an ongoing program or intensive format.

Small Group or 1-on-1 Multi-week program Test Prep
AP Exam Preparation

Targeted review for AP examinations in STEM subjects including Calculus AB/BC, Statistics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and Computer Science. Focus is on exam format fluency, free-response writing, and conceptual depth.

Small Group or 1-on-1 Seasonal intensive AP Courses
Academic Writing & Research Skills

Support for research papers, essays, and analytical writing across disciplines. Covers thesis development, source evaluation, citation practice, and revision strategies relevant to both coursework and college-level expectations.

1-on-1 60-min sessions Writing · Research

What Families Can Expect

  • Placement assessment and program recommendation at intake
  • Monthly written progress updates for families
  • Alignment to AP syllabi and school course schedules
  • Practice materials and review resources provided
  • Flexible scheduling including evenings and weekends
  • Consistent instructor for continuity across sessions

We recommend starting with an assessment to identify the most effective program fit.
